The Task: Drama in the Streets

Select any of the four roles from the Data Explorer. Imagine you are this person in 1870. A wealthy Londoner has just stopped to stare at you.

Write a 1-minute dramatic monologue directed at this stranger. You must weave the hard facts from your Data Card (wages, conditions, location) into your story to explain the reality of your daily survival.

Actor's Hints:

  • The Sweeper & Mudlark: Focus on the sheer physical toll of your environment, the cold, and the desperation for just a few pence.
  • The Clerk: Focus on middle-class anxiety. You walk for miles to avoid the omnibus fare, yet you are pressured to look perfectly respectable while dodging manure.
  • The Lamplighter: You are the observer. You stand exactly between the visible city of light and the invisible river of waste beneath your feet.

Letter to The Times
